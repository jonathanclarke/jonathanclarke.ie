Busy Friday.  Packed schedule, so I was on the treadmill early before the day swallowed me.  Training readiness at wake-up: 21.  Acute load at 329, that's 2.4x chronic, deep into overreaching territory.  Garmin's verdict: "Time to slow down."

The plan was a recovery jog with a strict 130 bpm heart rate cap.  2.8 kilometres in 25 minutes.  My kids could walk faster than this.

* **Distance:** 2.80 km
* **Time:** 25:01
* **Pace:** ~8:56 /km (avg)
* **Heart Rate:** 125 bpm (avg) / 151 bpm (max)
* **Calories:** 280 kcal
* **Feedback:** Improving Aerobic Base

Average heart rate of 125, so I mostly stayed under the cap.  That max of 151 is suspicious, either a spike during a pace change or the watch lost contact.  Body battery only drained 7 points from the whole session.  Barely counts as exercise, but that's the point.

### Sleep

Score of 73.  "Fair," but the best in two weeks, up from a 63 average.  Six hours nineteen minutes.  REM was low at 11.9%.  Still not enough sleep for someone pushing load this hard, but it's trending the right direction.

* **Deep sleep:** 63 min (16.6%) / **REM:** 45 min (11.9%) / **Light:** 4h 31m (71.5%)
* **Overnight HRV:** 47 ms / **Sleep stress:** 15

Resting heart rate dropped to 59 today, lowest I've seen recently.  Stress was almost nonexistent, average of 18, 88% of the day in rest state.  The body is recovering even if the readiness score hasn't caught up yet.

### The weekend plan

Training readiness after the jog: 10.  Recovery time estimate: 30.6 hours.  The algorithm wants me on the couch.

Last weekend I drank and it cost me four days of training.  Readiness cratered to 13, body battery was gutted, and I spent three sessions just clawing back to baseline.  Four days to undo one night.

This weekend I'm not.  Saturday is a full rest day, no alcohol, early to bed.  The plan is to actually give the body what it's been asking for all week instead of pouring beer on it.  If I can string together two decent nights of sleep, the readiness score should be in a completely different place by Sunday.

Seven hours of sleep would do more for my fitness age than any workout.  Time to test that theory.
